Hold & Stop a subscription

You asked for a way to pause (hold) or stop (deactivate) a subscription so it stops counting — instead of having to delete it. Good news: the data already supports both states; today they're hidden inside the Edit screen's status dropdown. This design pulls them out into two clear, one-tap actions on the subscription page — with an easy Resume to bring a held one back.

01Hold vs Stop — what each does

Two distinct actions so you're never guessing. Nothing is ever deleted — history and receipts stay.

⏸ On hold
Hold (pause) — a temporary stop. Reminders and the "did you pay?" check go quiet, and it drops out of your This-month-due and Monthly equivalent totals. It stays in the list, greyed, with a one-tap Resume. Optionally set an auto-resume date.
⏹ Stopped
Stop (deactivate) — you've ended this subscription for good. We stamp the end date, it leaves every spend total, and it's filed under Stopped. History & receipts are preserved; you can still Reactivate it later, or delete it outright if it was a mistake.
02On the subscription page

The action row gains Hold and Stop next to Edit. Tap either → a confirm sheet slides up.

04Exactly what changes
HoldSets status to paused. Drops out of This-month-due, Monthly equivalent & the active count. Reminders + post-due "did you pay?" checks pause. Optional auto-resume date. One tap to Resume.
StopSets status to cancelled and stamps endedOn. Removed from every spend total. Filed under Stopped. Reactivate brings it back to active.
Kept safeCycles, payment history & receipts are never touched — held/stopped only changes what's counted going forward, exactly like the spec's "history is sacred" rule.
Who canParents only (matches today's Edit/Delete). Property & Land subs mirrored from Wealth are managed there, as now.
DeleteStill there for "created by mistake" — but Hold/Stop is now the obvious choice for "I just want it to quit counting."
