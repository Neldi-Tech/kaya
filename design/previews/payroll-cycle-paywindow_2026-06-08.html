Two clean ideas: the cycle worked, and when it's paid

You're right on both counts. The fix is to stop mixing two different things. A salary now has a work cycle (the whole month worked) and a separate pay window (when you actually pay it). And Kaya raises it for you a few days before the cycle ends — not on the pay day.

Budget follows this

🗓️ Work cycle

The whole month worked — e.g. May 2026 (1–31 May). This is what the salary covers and which month's budget it lands in. Shown as the month, never a 5-day sliver.

Just when you pay

💸 Pay window

When you actually hand over the money — 1–5 June (or, for parents who pay in-month, e.g. by the 28th). Separate from the cycle; it only sets when “Mark paid” shows. It never moves the budget month.

① The entry reads cleanly now

No more “2026-05-01 → 2026-05-05”. The cycle is the month; the pay window is its own line; the badge tells you where it is.

What changes — exactly
Work cycleA salary covers the full month (May 1–31), shown as “May 2026 cycle”. Budget month follows the cycle. No more 5-day slivers.
Raise dateGenerated + auto-approved + posted to budget N days before the cycle ends (parent-set, default 7) — so May is raised ~24 May, not on 8 June.
Pay windowSeparate setting: Next month 1–5 or Same month by the Nth. Only controls when “Mark paid” shows; never moves the budget month.
Processing → PaidSits as Processing (in budget) until you tap Mark paid in the window, which captures the pay day.
No approval spamWith auto-approve on, salaries don't pile in “Awaiting your nod” — they're pre-approved to budget. (Turn off to keep a manual nod, still pre-month-end.)
CleanupStale/mis-raised rows get a one-tap remove; the new cadence won't regenerate them.
