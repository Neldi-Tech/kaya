Kaya · Module Proposal · v1 · 13 May 2026
Phase 1.5 · Kaya Business 🌱

Real businesses for real kids.

One business per kid. Five kid screens, one Parent Console. Sales credit Hive Cash; costs draw from a parent-funded float or the kid's wallet. Sunday auto-generated weekly P&L is visible to siblings. Every screen carries kid-friendly definitions so the vocabulary teaches itself. Designed for both mobile (kid-first) and web (parent console).

01

The Big Idea

Kaya Business is the operations layer for kid micro-enterprises. The Hive remains the money layer. Every sale a kid logs flows through Hive's parent-approval rules and lands in their Hive Cash (on-hand or on deposit). Every care task a kid completes earns House Points through the existing routine engine. Business doesn't reinvent money — it teaches it.

🌱

Venture-agnostic

An orchard, a chicken pen, a baking stand, an art shop, a tutoring side-hustle. One business per kid, with a flat list of assets — not nested ventures. Whatever the kid invents.

🍯

Wired into Hive

Sales credit Hive Cash with a destination of the kid's choice (👛 On Hand or 🏦 On Deposit). Costs draw from a parent-funded float or the kid's wallet. Care tasks earn HP through Hive routines. Single source of money truth.

📈

Teaches by doing

Kids learn the vocabulary of business by using it. Asset, profit, deposit, float, valuation — these are real grown-up words. Every screen has an inline "What's this?" definition card.

07

"What's this?" — the teaching pattern

Mobile + Web

Every screen carries a small What's this? card with the relevant kid-friendly definition. Kids tap to expand. The product teaches the vocabulary of business by using it — Asset, Profit, Deposit, Float, Valuation are real grown-up words, and kids should learn them in context, not avoid them.

My BusinessYour very own little company — the things you grow or make, the people you sell to, and the money you earn.
AssetSomething you own that helps you make money. Diella's vines, Earlnathan's hens.
StageWhere your asset is in its life. A vine starts as a seedling, then grows, then fruits.
Unit PriceHow much one of your assets is worth right now. A laying hen is worth more than a chick.
Live ValuationTotal worth of all your assets. Count × Unit Price.
SaleWhen you give something you made or grew to someone in exchange for money.
Family SaleA sale to someone in our house. The price is set in the Family Price List.
Relative SaleA sale to someone outside our house. You decide the price together.
CostMoney you spend to keep your business going — feed, fertilizer, sugar.
FloatMoney Mama or Papa puts into your business to help you start, so you don't use your own savings.
ProfitWhat's left after you take costs out of sales. Profit = Sales − Costs. If costs are bigger, that's a loss — and that's okay; it teaches us to plan.
The HiveYour money home inside Kaya. Holds House Points, Honey Coins, and Cash.
Cash on HandThe real money you carry — in your pocket. You can spend it.
DepositMoney you've handed to Mama or Papa to keep safe. You ask to take some out first.
Care ReminderA small job your asset needs to stay healthy. Doing them earns House Points.
Weekly ReportA summary every Sunday of how your business did. What sold? What's next?

Wiring · Business → Hive

System

Kaya Business never writes to a kid's wallet directly. Sales and wallet-funded costs become approval requests; Hive's existing flow does the actual ledger move. Float-funded costs stay inside Business — they don't touch Hive. Care tasks complete through Hive's existing routine engine.

09

Roadmap

v1 · NOW

Kaya Business — Phase 1

Five kid screens, one Parent Console, the Family Price List, the Cost Float, the Asset Library, sibling-visible Sunday reports, kid-friendly definitions on every screen.

  • One business per kid · flat asset list
  • Sales (family + relative) · Costs (float + wallet)
  • Care reminders → Hive routines → HP
  • Asset stages + lifecycle (loss / advance / retire / transfer)
  • Currency family-configured (TZS / USD / …)
v2 · NEXT

Polish & reach

Quality-of-life additions once the v1 pilot is real.

  • Photo evidence on production / sale logs
  • Push / WhatsApp care reminders
  • Joint ventures (two kids co-own a bakery)
  • Per-report privacy toggle
  • Optional global-profile USD translation
v3 · FUTURE

Marketplace

When kids outgrow the household.

  • Inter-venture trade (Earlnathan supplies eggs to Diella's bakery)
  • Real external customers (school, neighborhood)
  • Mobile-money rails (M-Pesa, Tigo Pesa)
  • Tax / profit-sharing concepts

v1 sign-off.

Five kid screens, one Parent Console, every locked decision from the brief reflected. Mobile-first for kids; web-first for the parent. Plumbing into Hive v3 (cash split + sale-approval) defined and consistent.
